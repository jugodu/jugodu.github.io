About me

Neuroscientist and Ph.D. candidate specializing in neuromodulation and deep brain stimulation, with a focus on the neural dynamics of involuntary behavior. My research spans adaptive DBS, biomarker discovery, and real-time brain sensing — combining clinical work with computational signal analysis and device programming. I bring prior industry experience at Medtronic (neuromodulation), Roche Diagnostics (technical operations), and Procter & Gamble (marketing/communication), enabling me to bridge engineering, patient needs, and product strategy.

Originally from Colombia and a J. William Fulbright Scholar, I'm seeking roles in neuromodulation, clinical research, neurophysiology, product development, or MedTech innovation.

Research

What I work on

Adaptive DBS for Tourette syndrome

Closed-loop neuromodulation using Medtronic Percept™ PC with SenSight directional leads, targeting CM thalamus and anterior GPi.

LFP signal processing

Bilateral LFP recording, artifact identification, and biomarker extraction for tic detection and aDBS feedback control.

Multimodal sensing

Synchronized EMG (Delsys), video, and FROST streams to characterize voluntary vs. involuntary movement in DBS patients.

Cardiac artifact analysis

Characterizing ECG contamination in bilateral LFP recordings for robust aDBS systems.
